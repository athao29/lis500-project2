Numbers Take Us Only So Far

Maxine Williams, Facebook’s Global Chief Diversity Officer, is a key figure in the push for greater diversity in the tech industry. She is known for her straightforwardness and strategic approach, making her a force in the diversity and inclusion space.

As one of Mark Zuckerberg and Sheryl Sandberg’s go-to executives, Williams has played a major role in Facebook’s diversity efforts. According to Facebook’s 2017 diversity update:

  • The number of women working at Facebook globally increased from 33% to 35%.
  • Women in technical roles rose from 17% to 19%.
  • Hispanic representation in the U.S. workforce increased from 4% to 5%.
  • Black representation grew from 2% to 3%.

Despite these improvements, Williams believes there is still a long way to go. “If you ask me when would I be satisfied, I’d be satisfied when it’s 50% [women at Facebook],” she told Glassdoor. “More, I always want more.”

Her mission goes beyond just increasing numbers—she wants Facebook’s workforce to truly reflect its over two billion users worldwide.
